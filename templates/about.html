About Bhartiya Boudh Mahasabha

The Bharatiya Bouddha Mahasabha is an organization founded by Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar in 1955 through Shri Wamanrao Godbole, Nagpur. It was registered in 1956 under the Society Act of 1860, Registration No. B-8, and in 1963 under the Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 (Bom. XXIX of 1950), Registration No. F-611(N). It is also listed in Schedule-1 of the Charity Commissioner, Nagpur. This organization is entirely different from the Buddhist Society of India (another registered organization formed by Dr. Ambedkar in 1955).

This organization was established to carry forward the Buddhist revolution initiated by Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar on 14th October 1956 at Deekshabhoomi. The movement is not only religious but also social. The objective is to unite people based on the values of equality, fraternity, and freedom as envisioned by Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar. There is no intention to undermine any organization.

Currently, the national president of the organization is Bhante Arya Nagarjun Surai Sasai, and the General Secretary is Shri Anand Tukaram Patil. The present office of the organization is at Plot No. 721, Tripitak Smruti, Buddhanagar, Nagpur – 440017.
